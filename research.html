Research Interests

I am broadly interested in applications of topological string theories to complex differential and algebraic geometry.
My Ph. D. thesis was under the supervision of David Morrison, and is titled ``Supersymmetric Topological Sigma Models and Doubling Spaces''. The thesis is available in the open access repository eScholarship, and can be found here.

My research is focused on topological string theories with extended supersymmetry, specifically the N=4 supersymmetric topological string theory proposed by Berkovits and Vafa. These theories naturally exist on manifolds with a hyperkähler structure, and encode invariants of the manifold such as Dolbeault cohomology groups with respect to the various complex structures. It is known that certain sectors of this theory compute the derived category of coherent sheaves on the manifold with respect to a chosen complex structure, and I am working on finding relations between these different sectors.

I am also working on incorporating the framework of generalized complex geometry into the N=4 model. Recently, computational tools for generalized complex geometry have been developed that allow for certain abstract quantities (e.g. cohomology of an exact Courant agebroid) to be computed in terms of more familiar quanities on a closely related geometric space. I am working on applying these tools in the hyperkähler setting to compute the various relevant categories in the N=4 model.

My thesis explored these topics in the context of abelian varieties. We show that a doubling construction, in the sense of Hull and Reid-Edwards' Double Field Theory, is a natural setting to study B-type D-branes on the abelian variety from a Lagrangian perspective. We establish that line bundles (rank-one space-filling D-branes) lift to linear Lagrangian torii in the doubled space, and the intersection theory of these torii is closely related to the topological open-string spectrum of the original theory.
